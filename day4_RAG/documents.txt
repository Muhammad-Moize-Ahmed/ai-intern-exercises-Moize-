Transformers use self-attention mechanisms to model relationships between all tokens in a sequence simultaneously.
Diffusion models generate images by gradually removing noise from a random starting point until a coherent image emerges.
Embeddings convert text into numerical vectors that capture the semantic meaning of the content.
Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) combines a retrieval system with a language model to answer questions using external documents.
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are designed for image recognition by learning spatial features through convolutional filters.
Reinforcement learning trains agents to take actions in an environment by maximizing a cumulative reward signal.
Large Language Models are pre-trained on massive text corpora and can generate coherent and contextually relevant text.
Vector databases store high-dimensional embeddings and enable fast similarity search across millions of documents.
Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors and is commonly used to compare text embeddings in semantic search.
Backpropagation is the algorithm used to compute gradients and update weights in a neural network during training.
